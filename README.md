***Get Started with your Analysis Section!***

***Analysis Section***

This section should enable you to gain a deep understanding of the problem you intend to solve and allow you to identify the objectives for the project. We recommend you use the following headings and develop these sections to complete your Analysis section:

**1 - The Problem**

What is the problem you are going to solve and why is this a relevant problem to you?

**2 - Primary User**

You need to identify a primary user for your project. This needs to be an actual person, and you need to conduct an interview with them. This section will include information about the user, and why they are qualified to be your primary user. You also need to include the transcript of an interview you have conducted with your primary user, where you ask them questions to develop an understanding about the solution you need to produce for them. Following the interview you should create a table of requirements from the primary user (These will inform your objectives later)

**3 - Perspective Users**

You need to identify a group of potential users for the solution you are planning. This needs to be a group, and not an individual. You should create a questionnaire to gain relevant feedback on what they feel your solution should include. As with the primary user section, this section should end with a table of requirements that will also inform your objectives.

**4 - Analysis Of Current Systems**

You should identify at least 2 solutions to your problem (or similar problem) that currently exist and analyse them. You need to explain these systems in detail, providing insight into how they deal with the problems you are going to have to solve in your project. You should also provide an analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of these systems in tabular format.

By looking at these systems you should begin to get an idea about how you might start designing and creating your project.

**5 - Research**

Your interviews and analysis could throw up areas that you need to research or develop your understanding in. This section is to prove that you have thoroughly thought about the problem and developed your understanding in order to consider the solution you will develop.

**6 - Objectives & Acceptable Limitations**

At this point you should have a clear idea about the solution you need to create. You need to create a list of objectives that your solution will be measured against. This section should detail the main aspects that your program needs to have in order to create a solution for your users. There may be aspects that are not fundamental to creating a functioning solution, but would be nice additions. These should be noted as Acceptable Limitations, so that if they are not complete they do not have an impact on your "Completeness" mark.

**7 - Modelling**

Now you have an idea about what you need to produce, and have done your initial research into what you may need to complete the project, you should have an initial idea about what you need to make. This section is an initial model of how your solution might work. Here you need to consider what the main parts of your project are going to be, and how they will interact with each other.


***Get Started with your Documented Design Section!***

***Documented Design Section***

This section should enable you to create a design for your solution that you can create a technical solution around. In essence anyone who is proficient in Python should be able to read your DD section and code a solution that is very similar to yours. This section should follow the principles of "High Level System Design", and be a section that refers to the overall system you are going to build and should include information about the architecture of your system, any database design and descriptions of systems, services, platforms and relationships between the different modules you plan to use in your solution. Successful DD sections usually include the following sections (Some are specific to problmes):

**Top Down Design**

What are the main parts of your solution, and how do you want them to work together? How are the different parts of your solution linked? This should be done in a diagramatical form, and could be similar to a hierarchy chart.

**Program Flow Diagram**

This diagram should illustrate the logical steps in your solution. What does each aspect of your solution actually do?

**User Interface Design**

How will your intended users interact with your solution? How will they navigate it, and what are the important user interactions you need to record program data? What interations do you expect, and how is this actually going to look. This should be drawn out and labelled, ensuring you explain what the interaction is, and what is happening to data in these interactions.

**Data Flow Diagram**

What data does your solution require, and what will be generated? How will this data flow around your solution, and how will it get passed around?

**Algorithm Design**

What algorithms does your solution require? What are they? How will they work? Consider the best way to present these algorithms. is it in a flow chart or psuedocode? DO NOT USE PYTHON HERE!!

**OOP Design**

If you are using OOP explain your classes, and definitely create a class diagram.

**Database Design**

If you are creating a database, how will you design it? Can you create a relationship diagram? What data dictionaries are you using?

**IPSO Chart**

Make sure you tabularise all of your solutions Inputs, Processes, Storage and Outputs

**Machine Learning Model**

If using AI, what machine learning model will you use, and how will you actually train it? Reference libraries you are using, and be explicit about what it actually is that YOU are creating to ensure you can demonstrate your own work.
